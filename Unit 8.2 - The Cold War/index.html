    Unit 8.2 The Cold War

    Establishment of the United Nations¶

    The League of Nations failed because of: - A lack of support from powerful nations, most notably the US. - There was no safeguard to quickly stop small conflicts to escalate into greater ones.

    US, GB, USSR, and China discussed the establishment of the UN, and it was formally established in 1945.

    Winston Churchill’s March 1946 speech:

    “An iron curtain has descended across the continent.”¶

    (This is where the establishment of the Iron Curtain metaphor begins.)

    Rivalries in Economics and Politics¶

    Communism vs Capitalism:¶

    Capitalism:¶

    • Individual economic freedoms for people
    • Means of production like factories and businesses are owned by private individuals
    • Private owners make decisions about what to produce, when to product, and how much should it cost
    • People can compete in the market freely without any government assistance
    • Theoretically, prices will be minimized because producers choose the cheapest product that’s the best
    • Goal: “The most deserving person will usually win.”
    • Supply and Demand: price drop -> demand goes up and supply decreases; prices only rise as long as demand is high.

    Communism:¶

    • No private ownership of property is allowed, Marx believed it encouraged greed.
    • Property should be shared.
    • “People” control the economy.
    • In the transition between capitalism and communism, the government should exercise control of the markets.
    • Goal: Eliminate wealth disparity between poor and rich.

    Democracy vs. Authoritarianism:¶

    United States:¶

    • elected leaders via free elections
    • independent press for accurate government info

    Soviet Union:¶

    • insignificant elections
    • press operated by the government
    • dominated by a single party

    Similarities and Differences:¶

    Similarities:¶

    • Big economic decisions was in the hands of groups (either the government or millions of corporate shareholders).
    • Both countries acted out of fear of each other.
    • Both absolutely hated Nazis.

    Differences:¶

    • USSR restricted freedom of speech and worship, free elections, and independent businesses.
    • USSR claimed the US gave people the “freedom to starve” and discriminating against minorities like African Americans.
    • USSR implemented equal rights for women.

    International Affairs¶

    Establishment of Satellite States:¶

    • The USSR enforced five-year economic plans in Eastern Europe to encourage the growth of industry and agriculture. Cost: Consumer products and all political parties besides Communism.
    • The satellite states were forced to import Soviet goods and export goods to the Soviet Union. Employed similar dictatorial governments.

    Definition - Satellite State:¶

    small states that are economically or politically dependent on a larger, more powerful state.

    After the October Revolution of 1917, the USSR saw capitalism as a global threat and developed the concept of a world revolution. Between 1917 and 1923, the USSR would support uprisings in Germany, Bavaria, Hungary, northern Italy, and Bulgaria.

    Containment:¶

    Some people supported the idea of violent overthrowing of regimes to destroy the spread of communism, while diplomat George Kennan (who worked in the US Embassy in Moscow between the 1930s and 1946) advocated for a policy of containment.

    March 1947 - Truman Doctrine:¶

    • Harry Truman declared that the spread of communism must be stopped in Greece and Turkey.
    • The USSR wanted to put military bases in Turkey to control the Dardanelles Strait between the Black Sea and the Mediterranean.
    • Left-wing groups in Greece were controlled by communists.
    • Truman urged for economic and military support for these two nations (about $400 Million).

    June 1947 - Marshall Plan:¶

    • For fear of the rise of Communism due to economic instability in European nations, America wanted to turn Europe into a stable and prosperous region.
    • America agreed to give $12 billion in aid to European nations, including Germany for modernization and repairment of damaged infrastructure.
    • Success: Economic output in aided countries was 35% higher in 1951 than in 1938.

    Jan 1949 - Council for Mutual Economic Assistance (COMECON):¶

    • A plan initiated by the Soviet Union to economically aid Eastern Bloc nations and facilitate trade among socialist states.
    • The idea was to prevent Eastern European nations from accepting Western aid.
    • The members were originally Soviet Union, Bulgaria, Czechoslovakia, Hungary, Poland, and Romania, but Albania joined in 1949 and East Germany joined in 1950.

    Space Race:¶

    • 1957: The Soviet Union launches the first Earth-orbiting satellite (Sputnik 1) and later the first organism in space (Sputnik 2, Laika the dog).
    • 1958: The US launches their first satellite (Explorer 1). The National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) is also created in this year.
    • 1961: Yuri Gagarin becomes the first man in space. Alan Shepard becomes the first American in space shortly after.
    • 1963: The USSR sends the first woman into space (Valentina Tereshkova).
    • 1969: Neil Armstrong and Edwin “Buzz” Aldrin become the first men to walk on the moon.

    In 1975, tensions soften between the US and USSR as they have a joint mission, in which commanders Tom Stafford and Alexei Leonov have an ‘international handshake,’ ending the Space Race.

    Mutually Assured Destruction:¶

    • In 1959, the US and USSR had developed intercontinental ballistic missiles capable of delivering warheads into each other’s territory. These nations realized that nuclear war would lead to total destruction, leading to a mutually assured destruction. Result: No incentive to start war.

    Non-Aligned Movement:¶

    • Many African and Asian countries wanted to not be involved with the Cold War, and wanted an alternative framework for political and economic order.
    • 1955 - Bandung Conference:
      • delegates from more than 27 countries came (more than 1/2 of world pop.)
      • Catalyst for the formal creation of the Non-Aligned Movement in 1961.

    • Problems with the Non-Aligned Movement:¶

      • In times of crises, many nations would align with either the US or USSR.
        • Example: 1977 war between Somalia and Ethiopia: USSR backed Ethiopia, prompting US to back Somalia.
      • Member states tried to use international institutions to project their own interests.
        • Example: Jawaharlal Nehru supported a stronger UN, but opposed efforts to intervene in Kashmir.
