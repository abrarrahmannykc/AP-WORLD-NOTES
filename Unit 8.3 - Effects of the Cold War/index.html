    The Division of Germany¶

    1945 - Germany and Berlin were divided into occupational zones between the Allies and the USSR.

    1948 - After the Marshall Plan, the Allies (France, US, and Great Britain) introduced the Deutschmark currency to counter the worthless Reichsmark, which the Soviets refused to accept. The Soviets then withdrew from the Allied Control Council.

    June 1948 - May 1949: Berlin Blockade¶

    • USSR severed water, railroad, and road access to Western sectors of Berlin.
    • To avoid military confrontation, the Allies established the Berlin Airlift, and between February 1948 and May 1949, the Allies flew supplies into Western Zones.

    Formation of NATO:¶

    The blockade also solidified Western unity, and alongside a growing idea to form a coordinated plan to stop Soviet expansion, Western Allies formed the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) in April 1949. Original Members: Belgium, Canada, Denmark, France, Great Britain, Iceland, Italy, Luxembourg, the Netherlands, Norway, Portugal, US.

    1949 - The Allies (France, US, and Great Britain) agreed to formally establish the nations of West Germany and East Germany.

    Between 1949 and 1961, around 2.5 million East Germans fled to the West in search for democracy. Since this mass fleeing demonstrated weakness in communist systems, the borders were heavily patrolled and around 150 people were killed. Barbed wires became an actual wall, known as the Berlin Wall, formed in August 1961.

    The fall of the Berlin Wall in 1989 demonstrated a new birth in European democracy as well as the end of Soviet influence.

    Other Alliances¶

    Warsaw Pact:¶

    • The Warsaw Pact was the USSR’s response to NATO, established in 1955.
    • Members: Albania, Bulgaria, Czechoslovakia, East Germany, Hungary, Poland, Romania, USSR
      • Resistance:¶
        • Albania withdrew in 1968 as it became more closely tied with China.
        • Yugoslavia, under the leadership of Marshall Josip Broz Tito never joined the Warsaw Pact. However, Yugoslavia split in the 1990s.

    **1954 - Formation of the Southeast Asia Treaty Organization (SEATO) - Members: Australia, France, Great Britain, New Zealand, Pakistan, Philippines, Thailand, US - Aimed to prevent the spread of Communism in Southeast Asia.

    **1959 - Formation of the Central Treaty Organization (CENTO) - Members: Iran, Iraq, Pakistan, Turkey -> Iraq eventually withdrew - Aimed to prevent the spread of Communism in the Middle East.

    Proxy Wars:¶

    Definition - Proxy War¶

    An indirect conflict where major powers fuel battles by assisting smaller nations to fight on their behalf.

    The threat of nuclear war kept the US and USSR from direct, global destruction, but nonetheless, proxy wars between the US and USSR still resulted in the deaths of millions.

    Korean War (1950 - 1953):¶

    • 1950 - North Korea attempts to invade South Korea to reunite under its leadership. UN responds by voting to defend South Korea militarily. Soviets did not choose to veto due to a protest in Security Council meetings over China’s seat.
    • The North was lead by Kim Il-Sung (communist government) and the South was lead by Syngman Rhee, an authoritarian leader backed by the US.
      • 16 member countries militarily aided South Korea.
    • General Douglas MacArthur was the lead commander for the US, directing the Inchon landing, the defense of the Busan perimeter, and the invasion of North Korea.
      • MacArthur hinted at the use of nuclear weapons or attacking China, and so Truman dismissed him for insubordination on April 1951.

    Definition - Limited War¶

    A war in which the weapons used, the nations or territory involved, or the objectives pursued are restricted in some way, in particular one in which the use of nuclear weapons is avoided.

    • Soviets aided NK economically rather than militarily, but China joined in out of concern that the UN peacekeeping forces would try to invade it.
    • STALEMATE - establishment of the demilitarized zone.

    Angola:¶

    • After around 400 years of Portuguese rule, resistance against colonialism started around 1961, starting the Angolan Independence War (1961-1974), lead by three main groups.
      • MPLA (Popular Movement for the Liberation of Angola)
        • Marxist-aligned, supported by USSR and Cuba
      • FNLA (National Front for the Liberation of Angola)
        • Supported by US and Zaire
      • UNITA (National Union for the Total Independence of Angola)
        • Aided by South Africa and the US
    • Angola formally received independence on November 11, 1975. However, the MPLA took control of the capital, Luanda, on the same month.
    • The Angolan Civil War lasted from 1975 to 2002, with conflict between the MPLA and UNITA.
      • large destruction to infrastructure
      • large social inequalities between urban and rural areas
      • land ownership disputes (still ongoing)
      • ceasefire after death of UNITA leader

    Contra War, Nicaragua:¶

    • The 43 year dictatorship by the Somoza family was ended by the rebel Sandinistas, self-proclaimed socialists (1979).
    • 1981 - The Contras, conservative opponents of the Sandinistas, tried to overthrow them, leading to the Contra War (1981-1988).
      • The US heavily supported the Contras since the Sandinistas were socialist. The USSR provided nearly $3 billion in military equipment to aid the Sandinista. Cuba also helped them.
