
## <u>Historical Context</u>
**1952** - Dictator Fulgencio Batista seizes control of Cuba via a coup, creating an authoritarian regime.
- high unemployment
- reliance on sugar exports
- wealth disparity
- US had strong control on Cuban economy

### Unit 8.3 - Effects of the Cold War

**Fidel Castro**, **Che Guevara**, and other revolutionaries led the revolutionary forces. Their guerilla forces gained popularity. Eventually, Fidel Castro gains control of Cuba and executes supports of Batista.
- Note: Castro was a dictator.

**1960** - Cuba nationalized foreign-owned industries, including **US investments and sugar mills.**
As a result, the US broke trade and diplomatic ties with Cuba, and Cuba starts accepting Soviet aid.

**1961** - Cuban exiles plead to JFK for an invasion of Cuba, known as the **Bay of Pigs Invasion**. **Result:** Total failure. Led to a strong bond between the USSR and Cuba.

## <u>Cuban Missile Crisis</u>
- Soviet Premier Nikita Khrushchev came to power after Stalin’s death in 1953, and supplied Cuba with arms during the **Bay of Pigs Invasion.**
- In the summer of 1961, the US placed nuclear missiles in Turkey.
- **1962** - Nikita ships nuclear missiles to Cuba -> U.S. intelligence finds out about this in October and launches a “quarantine.” 
	- Note: Naming this a blockade would be considered an act of war.
- #### The Cooling:
	- Nikita calls back Soviet ships and removes missiles deliver to Cuba.
	- US removes its missiles from Turkey.
- Establishment of the **Hot Line**, a direct telegraph link between US and USSR offices.
