

## 1. The Road to World War I (The Fuse)

- **1882** – **The Triple Alliance Formed**: Germany, Austria-Hungary, and Italy sign a mutual defense pact, splitting Europe into armed camps.
    
- **1906** – **Launch of HMS Dreadnought**: Great Britain launches a revolutionary warship, kicking the Anglo-German naval arms race (Militarism) into overdrive.
    
- **1907** – **The Triple Entente Formed**: Alarmed by Germany's growth, Great Britain, France, and Russia align.
    
- **June 28, 1914** – **The Spark 💥**: Serbian nationalist Gavrilo Princip assassinates Archduke Franz Ferdinand in Sarajevo.
    
- **July 1914** – **The July Crisis**: A diplomatic domino effect occurs as alliances activate. Austria-Hungary declares war on Serbia; Russia mobilizes.
    

---

## 2. World War I (1914–1918)

- **August 1914** – **Outbreak of War**: Germany enacts the Schlieffen Plan, invading neutral Belgium to strike France. Great Britain enters the war.
    
- **Late 1914** – **The Race to the Sea**: Both sides dig in on the Western Front, establishing a permanent, brutal system of **Trench Warfare** and strategic stalemate.
    
- **1915** – **The Armenian Genocide Begins**: Under the cover of total war, the Ottoman Empire begins the systematic deportation and massacre of its Christian Armenian minority.
    
- **1915** – **Industrial Carnage Escalates**: First massive use of poison gas at the Second Battle of Ypres. The sinking of the passenger liner _Lusitania_ by German U-boats strains US neutrality.
    
- **1916** – **The Battles of Attrition**:
    
    - _Battle of Verdun_: The longest battle of the war, resulting in over 700,000 casualties.
        
    - _Battle of the Somme_: The British introduce the tank to break the stalemate; over 1 million men are killed or wounded.
        
- **March 1917** – **The Russian Collapse (Part I)**: Severe food shortages and economic ruin force Tsar Nicholas II to abdicate during the February Revolution.
    
- **April 1917** – **US Entry**: Provoked by unrestricted submarine warfare and the Zimmermann Telegram, the United States declares war on Germany.
    
- **November 1917** – **The Bolshevik Revolution**: Vladimir Lenin and the communist Bolsheviks seize power in Russia, promising "Peace, Land, and Bread."
    
- **March 1918** – **Treaty of Brest-Litovsk**: Soviet Russia officially exits WWI, ceding massive amounts of territory to Germany.
    
- **November 11, 1918** – **Armistice Day 🕊️**: Germany, facing economic collapse and imminent military defeat, signs an armistice. The guns fall silent at 11:00 AM.
    

---

## 3. The Interwar Period (1919–1938)

- **June 1919** – **Treaty of Versailles**: The Allies impose harsh peace terms on Germany (War Guilt Clause, crippling reparations). The **League of Nations** is created but lacks enforcement power.
    
- **May 4, 1919** – **The May Fourth Movement**: Angry Chinese students protest the Versailles decision to award German territory in China to Japan, accelerating Chinese nationalism.
    
- **1922** – **Rise of Fascism in Italy**: Benito Mussolini leads the "March on Rome" and establishes a fascist dictatorship. The Ottoman Empire is officially abolished, replaced by Atatürk's secular Republic of Turkey in 1923.
    
- **1923** – **German Hyperinflation**: Economic collapse forces Germany to overprint currency, rendering the German Mark completely worthless.
    
- **October 1929** – **Black Tuesday 📉**: The US Stock Market crashes, triggering the **Great Depression** and destabilizing global free-market economies.
    
- **1928–1932** – **Stalin's First Five-Year Plan**: Joseph Stalin enforces the collectivization of agriculture in the USSR, leading directly to the man-made **Holodomor** famine in Ukraine (1932–1933).
    
- **1931** – **Invasion of Manchuria**: Imperial Japan invades northern China for natural resources. The League of Nations objects but does nothing.
    
- **1933** – **Hitler Seizes Power**: Adolf Hitler is appointed Chancellor of Germany, quickly dismantling democracy to establish the totalitarian Third Reich.
    
- **1935** – **Invasion of Ethiopia**: Fascist Italy invades Ethiopia. The League of Nations fails to intervene effectively.
    
- **1936** – **Remilitarization of the Rhineland**: Hitler openly defies the Treaty of Versailles by marching troops into the French border zone. The West watches.
    
- **September 1938** – **The Munich Conference (Appeasement)**: Britain and France agree to let Hitler annex the Sudetenland (Czechoslovakia) in exchange for a promise of no future expansion.
    

---

## 4. World War II (1939–1945)

- **August 1939** – **The Nazi-Soviet Pact**: Shocking alliance between Hitler and Stalin; secretly plans the partition of Poland.
    
- **September 1, 1939** – **The Invasion of Poland 🚀**: Germany unleashes **Blitzkrieg** tactics on Poland. Britain and France declare war. WWII begins.
    
- **June 1940** – **The Fall of France**: Nazi forces rapidly bypass the Maginot Line, conquering France in weeks.
    
- **Late 1940** – **The Battle of Britain**: The Royal Air Force successfully holds off a relentless German bombing campaign, preventing a Nazi invasion of Great Britain.
    
- **June 1941** – **Operation Barbarossa**: Hitler betrays Stalin and launches a massive invasion of the Soviet Union, opening the brutal Eastern Front.
    
- **December 7, 1941** – **Pearl Harbor**: Japan launches a surprise attack on the US naval base in Hawaii. The United States officially enters World War II.
    
- **1941–1942** – **The Holocaust Escalates**: The Nazi regime transitions from institutionalized discrimination to the "Final Solution," building industrial extermination camps like Auschwitz-Birkenau.
    
- **June 1942** – **Battle of Midway 🌊**: The US Navy intercepts Japanese codes and sinks four Japanese aircraft carriers, marking the turning point of the war in the Pacific.
    
- **Feb 1943** – **Battle of Stalingrad**: The Soviet Red Army forces the surrender of the German 6th Army. This catastrophic defeat marks the turning point of the war in Europe; Germany begins a steady retreat.
    
- **1943** – **Island Hopping Strategy Begins**: US forces selectively target and seize strategic islands across the Pacific to establish forward airfields.
    
- **June 6, 1944** – **D-Day (The Normandy Landings)**: Allied forces launch the largest amphibious invasion in history, opening up the Western Front in France.
    
- **May 8, 1945** – **V-E Day 🎉**: Following the capture of Berlin by Soviet forces and Hitler's suicide, Germany unconditionally surrenders.
    
- **August 6 & 9, 1945** – **The Atomic Bombs**: The US drops atomic weapons on Hiroshima and Nagasaki to force a rapid surrender and avoid a land invasion.
    
- **September 2, 1945** – **V-J Day**: Japan formally surrenders aboard the _USS Missouri_, bringing World War II to an official close.
    

---

## 5. The Immediate Aftermath & The Shift

- **October 1945** – **Founding of the United Nations**: Replaces the failed League of Nations, featuring a Security Council with enforcement powers to maintain international stability.
    
- **1946–1947** – **The Iron Curtain Descends**: The temporary alliance between the democratic Western Allies and the communist Soviet Union fractures, instantly creating the geopolitical landscape of the **Cold War**.
