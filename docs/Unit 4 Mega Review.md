
# AP World History Unit 4 Comprehensive Timeline: Transoceanic Interconnections (1450–1750)

Unit 4 tracks how the isolation of the Americas ended, creating the first truly global networks of exchange. This massive chronological timeline maps every major political development, technological shift, economic system, and cultural disruption required for the course.

---

## 🧭 Phase 1: Technological Foundations & Maritime Exploration (1400s–1510s)

- **1415**: **Portugal Captures Ceuta** – Prince Henry the Navigator triggers the Age of Discovery, funding maritime schools to find an ocean route to Asian spice markets.
    
- **1450s**: **Introduction of the Caravel & Astrolabe** – European mariners synthesize classical Greek, Islamic, and Asian technology. They adopt the lateen sail, sternpost rudder, magnetic compass, and astronomical charts to navigate the open Atlantic.
    
- **1488**: **Bartolomeu Dias Rounds the Cape of Good Hope** – Portuguese sailors prove that the Atlantic and Indian Oceans connect, opening an eastern maritime route to Asia.
    
- **1492**: **Columbus Reaches the Caribbean / The Reconquista Concludes** – Spain funds Christopher Columbus’s voyage, accidentally triggering the **Columbian Exchange**. Simultaneously, Spain expels Jewish and Muslim populations, consolidating Catholic state power.
    
- **1494**: **Treaty of Tordesillas** – The Pope draws a meridian line dividing the non-Christian world. Spain claims everything to the west (the Americas); Portugal claims everything to the east (Brazil and the African coast).
    
- **1498**: **Vasco da Gama Arrives in Calicut, India** – Portugal successfully enters the Indian Ocean network, establishing a maritime **Trading Post Empire** using armed caravels to enforce monopolies (_Cartaz_ permits).
    
- **1511**: **Portuguese Capture Malacca** – Alfonso de Albuquerque seizes the crucial choke point connecting the Indian Ocean to the South China Sea, choking out local Islamic merchant networks.

## ⚔️ Phase 2: Conquest, Colonization, and Global Trade Webs (1519–1580s)

- **1519–1521**: **Conquest of the Aztec Empire** – Hernán Cortés exploits indigenous rivalries, utilizes firearms, and relies on a devastating smallpox outbreak to topple Tenochtitlan and establish New Spain.
    
- **1521**: **Ferdinand Magellan’s Expedition Circumnavigates the Globe** – Spain maps the Pacific Ocean, claiming the Philippines and creating a continuous around-the-world trade route.
    
- **1532–1572**: **Conquest of the Inca Empire** – Francisco Pizarro captures Atahualpa at Cajamarca. Despite the indigenous **Neo-Inca State** resisting from Vilcabamba until 1572, Spanish colonial governance consolidates.
    
- **1545**: **Discovery of Silver at Potosí (Modern Bolivia)** – The Spanish unearth the world's largest silver deposit. To mine it, they co-opt the traditional Incan _Mit'a_ system into a deadly form of forced labor.
    
- **1565**: **Inauguration of the Manila Galleons** – Spain begins shipping American silver directly across the Pacific from Acapulco to Manila, exchanging it for Chinese silk, porcelain, and tea. This officially links the global economy through currency.
    
- **1570s**: **The Qing Dynasty Swallows Silver** – China institutes the **Single Whip Tax Law**, requiring all taxes to be paid in silver. This cements global demand for silver, tying Chinese economic stability directly to American extraction.

## ⛓️ Phase 3: Forced Labor, Sugar, and Joint-Stock Companies (1600s–1650s)

- **1600**: **Establishment of the British East India Company (EIC)** – Queen Elizabeth I grants a royal charter to a private joint-stock company, allowing it to raise private armies, print money, and colonize trade zones.
    
- **1602**: **Establishment of the Dutch East India Company (VOC)** – The Dutch launch the world’s first publicly traded mega-corporation. They use corporate warfare to displace the Portuguese and monopolize Southeast Asian nutmeg, cloves, and mace.
    
- **1607**: **Founding of Jamestown** – England establishes its first permanent colony in North America, funded by the Virginia Company, initiating tobacco cash-crop plantation economies.
    
- **1608**: **Founding of Quebec** – Samuel de Champlain establishes New France, building an economic empire centered around the global fur trade rather than cash-crop agriculture.
    
- **1619**: **First African Laborers Documented in Virginia** – The arrival of enslaved Africans in English colonies marks the early expansion of **Chattel Slavery** in North America.
    
- **1620s**: **The Explosion of the Sugar Plantation Complex** – Portuguese Brazil and Caribbean colonies run by the British, French, and Dutch transform into intensive sugar operations. High mortality rates among indigenous workers from disease prompt the rapid growth of the **Transatlantic Slave Trade**.
    
- **1637**: **Japan Institutes the Sakoku Edict** – Fearing Western gun diplomacy and Christian subversion (highlighted by the _Shimabara Rebellion_), the Tokugawa Shogunate seals Japan's borders. Foreigners are banned, and international trade is restricted exclusively to Dutch merchants confined to the artificial island of Dejima.

## 💥 Phase 4: Resistance, Revolts, and State Consolidation (1650s–1750)

- **1652**: **Dutch East India Company Founds Cape Colony** – The VOC establishes a permanent settler station at the southern tip of Africa to resupply merchant fleets moving between Europe and Asia.
    
- **1656–1683**: **The Fronde & Louis XIV’s Versailles** – French aristocratic revolts fail. Louis XIV consolidates absolute rule, stripping regional nobles of independent military power and forcing them to live under his surveillance at Versailles.
    
- **1675–1678**: **King Philip’s War (Metacom’s Rebellion)** – Native American forces in New England launch a unified military effort to expel English settlers. It stands as the deadliest conflict per capita in early American colonial history.
    
- **1680**: **The Pueblo Revolt** – Led by the medicine man Popé, indigenous Pueblo communities in modern New Mexico launch an organized uprising against Spanish colonial rule, successfully expelling Spanish forces and burning Catholic churches to protect their traditional religion for over a decade.
    
- **1689**: **The Glorious Revolution & The English Bill of Rights** – William and Mary take the throne, permanently securing a constitutional monarchy, protecting merchant property rights, and empowering Parliament over absolute monarchs.
    
- **1690s**: **Rise of the Maroon Communities** – Enslaved people who successfully escaped bondage form independent, fortified inland societies (such as Queen Nanny’s Maroons in Jamaica or Palmares in Brazil), maintaining guerrilla warfare against colonial forces.
    
- **1700s**: **Consolidation of the Casta System** – To maintain control over a highly mixed global population, Spanish Americas implement a strict racial hierarchy based on parentage:
    
    1. _Peninsulares_ (European-born elites)
        
    2. _Criollos/Creoles_ (Euro-descendants born in America)
        
    3. _Mestizos_ (Euro-Indigenous)
        
    4. _Mulattoes_ (Euro-African)
        
    5. _Indios_ & _Negros_ (Indigenous and African populations)
        
- **1703**: **Peter the Great Founds St. Petersburg** – The Russian Empire modernizes and westernizes its military and administrative layout, forcing its traditional boyar nobility to shave their beards and adopt Western dress code models.

### 💸 The Triangular Trade Dynamics

Industrial-era mercantilism created an economic circuit across the Atlantic:

- **Europe to Africa**: Manufactured consumer goods, firearms, and textiles.
    
- **Africa to the Americas (The Middle Passage)**: Enriched human cargo of enslaved labor.
    
- **The Americas to Europe**: Raw industrial commodities, sugar, tobacco, silver, and cotton.
