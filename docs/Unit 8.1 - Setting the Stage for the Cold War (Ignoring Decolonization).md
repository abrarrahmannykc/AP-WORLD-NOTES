
## Meetings Between the Big Three
The Big Three were the leaders of Great Britain, the Soviet Union, and the United States.
#### November 1943: Tehran Conference
*Plans for freeing Europe from Nazi control*
- Soviet Union would free Eastern Europe and gain some Polish territory
- Britain and the United States would free Western Europe

#### February 1945: Yalta Conference
*Plans for reconstructing Europe and defeating Japan*
- FDR wanted democratic elections and Soviets to join the war against Japan
- Stalin wanted an Eastern European ‘buffer zone’ to prevent another Russian invasion. He also wanted Japanese islands, Chinese ports, and Manchurian railroads.
**Key Detail**: The Soviet Union did not fully agree to free elections in Eastern Europe, but FDR believed that a potential war with the Soviet Union was deemed unpopular.

*FDR dies on April 12, 1945 -> Replaced by Harry Truman*
*Clement Atlee replaced Winston Churchill on July 26, 1945*
#### July 1945: Potsdam Conference
*Plans for the administration of post-Nazi Germany, as well as a strategy for ending the war against Japan*
- Truman also wanted democratic elections, but Stalin had already occupied Eastern Europe by then. **Effect:** Communists gained control of ***East Germany, Poland, Czechoslovakia, Hungary, Bulgaria, and Romania***
- Failed to settle major differences between the global superpowers, and so a Cold War Begins.

#### Utter Destruction of Europe:
- Poland, Germany, and the Soviet Union lost between 10-20% of its population.
- Immense physical destruction and population loss reduced global power from Europe, and the United States and the Soviet Union emerged as superpowers.
- Eastern Europe suffered harder than Western Europe. Many educational institutions and innovative corporations remained intact in France and Britain.

### Growing Rivalry Between US and USSR:
- The United States suffered the least damage from WW2, and its industrial base grew stronger from military contracts.
- The success from the United States allowed it to fund many aid programs to European nations.
- The US were the frontrunners of the development of the atomic bomb, and the USSR only developed theirs in 1949.
- Neither nation wanted a full-scale war with each other due to the heavy costs of WW2. They ‘fought’ via propaganda campaigns, arms races, and covert operations.
- The US and USSR developed hydrogen bombs in the 1950s, more powerful than the atomic bomb. 
- Dwight D. Eisenhower warned against the military-industrial complex, for its unchecked power could drive financial incentives toward unnecessary conflict. **Result:** Citizens in many countries protested the stockpiling of nuclear weapons.

##### Definition: Military-Industrial Complex
The informal alliance between the government and the large defense contractors.
