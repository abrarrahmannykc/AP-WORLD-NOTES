
## 1. Causes of the Global Environmental Crisis

The 20th and 21st centuries saw unprecedented environmental change driven by globalization and industrialization.

- **Industrial Activity**: Large-scale manufacturing has led to widespread environmental contamination.
    
- **Resource Extraction**: Illegal operations like logging, mining, and hunting have caused massive environmental destruction and species endangerment.
    
- **Agricultural Practices**: Overfarming and deforestation have depleted natural resources.
    
- **Carbon Emissions**: Increased use of fossil fuels has significantly raised carbon emissions.
    
    - Top global emitters include companies like Saudi Aramco, Chevron, Gazprom, and ExxonMobil.
        
- **Political Factors**: Lax environmental legislation and intense corporate lobbying have hindered protective measures.
    

---

## 2. Deforestation and Biodiversity Loss

- **Population Pressure**: A rapidly increasing global population has created a need for more living space, leading to the clearing of forests.
    
- **Loss of Habitat**: Since forests are critical habitats, their destruction has caused many species to become endangered or extinct, resulting in significant biodiversity loss.
    
- **Climate Link**: Trees act as carbon sinks; their removal accelerates climate change by leaving more CO2 in the atmosphere.
    

---

## 3. Climate Change and Melting Ice Caps

- **Greenhouse Gases**: Human-induced climate change is driven by the rise of gases such as carbon dioxide (CO2), methane, and nitrous oxide.
    
- **Melting Ice Sheets**: Global warming is causing ice caps to melt worldwide.
    
- **Consequences**:
    
    - **Habitat Loss**: Species living on polar ice (e.g., polar bears) are losing their environments.
        
    - **Sea Level Rise**: Melting ice leads to rising seawater levels and increased global flooding.
        
- **Historical Context**: Much of the current crisis is traced back to technological advances from the Industrial Revolution, including cars, factories, and the high demand for modern consumer goods like plastic and electronics.
    

---

## 4. The Ozone Hole Crisis

- **The Ozone Layer**: A layer of O3 (oxygen) that protects the Earth from harmful solar radiation.
    
- **Discovery**: In 1985, scientists formally recognized a hole forming in the ozone layer over Antarctica.
    
- **Cause**: 1970s technologies like refrigeration and aerosol deodorants used **chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs)**.
    
    - When CFCs are hit by UV light, they break down ozone faster than it can naturally replenish.
        
- **Montreal Protocol (1987)**: An international treaty widely adopted to phase out CFCs; it is often cited as the most successful example of global environmental cooperation.
    

---

## 5. Responses and Movements

- **Political Action**: Green Parties have formed in nations like the US and UK to lobby for stronger environmental laws.
    
- **Green Belt Movement (1977)**: Founded by Wangari Maathai in Kenya, this movement focuses on teaching women to plant trees to combat deforestation and desertification.
    
- **International Agreements**:
    
    - **Kyoto Protocol (1997)** and **Paris Agreement (2015)** aimed to address global climate change through international cooperation.
        
- **Greenpeace**: An NGO founded in 1971 that uses localized activism to push for corporate accountability and better legislation.
    
- **Proposed Policies**: The "Green New Deal" in the US is a proposed framework to mitigate environmental issues through policy change.
    
- **Public Awareness**: Climate strikes, scientific outreach, and documentaries have reinforced the perceived threat of environmental collapse.
    

---

## 6. Sustainable Alternatives: Renewable Energy

Global efforts have shifted toward using energy sources that are replenished at a higher rate than they are consumed.

- **Benefits**: Renewable sources create far lower emissions and are more environmentally friendly than fossil fuels.
    
- **Examples**: Solar, wind, geothermal, hydropower, and bioenergy.
