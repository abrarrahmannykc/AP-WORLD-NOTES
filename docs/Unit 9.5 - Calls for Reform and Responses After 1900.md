
## 1. An Era of Rights: Global Standards

In the wake of World War II, the international community sought to establish universal standards for human dignity.

- **Universal Declaration of Human Rights (1948)**:
    
    - Established by the United Nations.
        
    - Asserted basic rights and fundamental freedoms for **all** humans regardless of race, gender, religion, or nationality.
        
    - The UN investigates abuses such as genocide, war crimes, and government oppression.
        

## 2. United Nations Specialized Agencies

- **UNICEF (UN International Children's Emergency Fund)**:
    
    - Originally created to provide food for children after WWII.
        
    - Today: The world's largest provider of vaccines; provides education, health, and nutrition services for children globally.
        
- **UNHCR (UN Refugee Agency)**:
    
    - Provides food, medicine, and shelter to refugees (e.g., assisted refugees during the 1948 UN partition of Palestine).
        
- **International Court of Justice (World Court)**:
    
    - Settles legal disputes between countries (e.g., border disputes and treaty violations).
        

## 3. Global Feminism and Women's Rights

The 20th century saw a massive shift in the social and political status of women.

- **Suffrage**: Women gained the right to vote in most parts of the world (e.g., US in 1920, UK in 1918/1928, Brazil in 1932).
    
- **Education**: Literacy rates for women rose globally, leading to increased participation in the workforce.
    
- **CEDAW (1979)**: The Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women—often described as an international bill of rights for women.
    

## 4. Racial Equality and Anti-Apartheid Movements

- **Apartheid in South Africa**: A system of institutionalized racial segregation and discrimination.
    
- **Nelson Mandela**: Leader of the African National Congress (ANC). Though imprisoned for 27 years, his leadership and international pressure (boycotts/sanctions) eventually led to the end of Apartheid.
    
- **1994 Election**: Mandela became the first Black president of South Africa in the nation's first fully representative democratic election.
    
- **Caste Discrimination**: In India, the 1950 Constitution officially banned discrimination against **Dalits** (formerly "untouchables"), though social challenges remain.
    

## 5. Environmental and Economic Reform

- **Green Belt Movement (1977)**:
    
    - Founded by **Wangari Maathai** in Kenya.
        
    - Empowered women to plant trees to combat deforestation and soil erosion.
        
    - Linked environmental conservation with women’s rights and political accountability.
        
- **Greenpeace**: A multinational agency (now in 55+ countries) that advocates for the prevention of environmental destruction.
    
- **Fair Trade**:
    
    - The **World Fair Trade Organization (WFTO)** was formed to prioritize people's rights over profits.
        
    - Focused on: Abolishing child labor, ensuring fair wages for farmers, and promoting sustainable farming.
        

## 6. Post-Communist Transitions

- **End of the Cold War**: As the USSR dissolved into 15 independent nations, many states transitioned from **Command Economies** (government-controlled) to **Market Economies** (private-sector-driven).
    
- **Protests**: These transitions were often accompanied by social unrest and protests as people pushed for more democratic and transparent policies.
