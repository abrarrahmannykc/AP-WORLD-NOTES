## <u>Historical Context Leading Up to the Civil War </u>
- The Qing Dynasty was replaced by a nationalist government in 1911 (birth of the Republic of China). Old-school Confucians were not supportive of this. 
	- Party leader Sun Yat Sen becomes Prime Minister.
- **1911-1912** - Eventually, Sun Yat Sen steps down and let Yuan Shikai be president of the ROC to prevent the country from being torn apart.
	- Yuan Shikai was a dictator, dissolving the parliament and banning the KMT. He even declared himself as emperor. **Dies in 1916.**
- Although the ROC essentially split into many different warlord factions, the government still maintained its legitamacy diplomatically. Thus, Sun Yat-Sen and Mao Zedong formed the **First United Front in 1923** to end warlordism in China, with the pact ending in **1927**.
- Sun Yat Sen **dies** in 1925 and Chiang Kai-shek replaces him. He ordered the purge of communists after the warlordism period (known as the **Shanghai Massacre** in 1927), which started the “First” Chinese Civil War.
- The CCP was forced into a massive retreat known as the **Long March (1934-1935)** to seek refuge against the Nationalist forces. This gained the respect of many peasants in China.
- After Chiang Kai-shek’s kidnapping during the *Xi’an Incident,* he agrees to form the **Second United Front (1937-1945)**.

 ## Unit 8.4 - Spread of Communism After 1900
### Chinese Civil War (1945-1949)
- The Chinese Civil War immediately continued after the end of WW2 in 1945.
- Mao Zedong was already popular among the peasants because:
	- he had redistributed land to peasants before
	- opened schools and hospitals
	- punished soldiers who mistreated civilians
- The peasants generally saw the Nationalists as more corrupt.
- The “China White Paper” is a US publication that explains US policy during the Chinese Civil War. It explained that the “loss of China” to the CCP was beyond American interests.
	- **Result:** Americans believed that the US abandoned an ally. Mao believed the paper slandered the CCP.
- Chiang Kai-shek and the KMT fled to Taiwan in December 1949, ultimately losing to the People’s Liberation Army.

### Sino-Soviet Split (1961-1980s)

After the **Death of Stalin** in **1953**, the Soviet Union underwent ‘Destalinization,’ and Nikita Khrushchev replaced him as leader.
- Cities were renamed 
- many hundreds of thousands of prisoners were released from the Gulag
- Loosening of censorship
- Slight economic shift towards western alliances and more consumer goods

Mao condemned the USSR’s destalinization, leading to the **Sino-Soviet Split**. Mao did not the USSR’s “peaceful coexistence” with the west and feared his authority in the CCP as Stalin failed to establish a cult of personality.
There were many border clashes between the USSR and China during the 1960s-1980s.

### Great Leap Forward (1958-1962)
- The **Great Leap Forward** aimed for a more ambitious leap into communism that that of the **Five-Year Modernization Plan**, as Mao wanted to surpass Western economies within years.
- It was a plan to transform China from an agrarian economy into a modern industrial economy.
- Around **100 Million peasants** were redirected from agriculture to infrastructure, which caused crops to be neglected.
- Peasants were organized into central communes for agriculture, which led to inefficient farming.
- Millions of homemade, backyard steel furnaces were built using inefficient fuels to product low-quality iron.

#### Four Pest Policy:
- targeted mosquitos, rats, flies, and sparrows
	- Killing sparrows led to massive growth in crop-eating insects (like locusts).

**Results:** Largest famine in human history, between 20-40 million deaths. The ecosystem was damaged due to the mass killing of sparrows. The economy was severely disrupted.

### The Cultural Revolution (1966-1976)
Mao publishes his **Little Red Book** in 1962, which contained famous sayings and ideas from Mao. The intended audience was the workers and the youth.

Mao also swims the Yangtze River to display its superior quality in 1966 (though the truth to that has been disputed).

The Cultural Revolution saw a:
- mass persecution of intellectuals
- elimination of traditional beliefs like Confucianism
- mass killing/reassignment of people to labor camps for “re-education”
- stagnation of education, as many young people were sent to do labor and children turned against parents and students turned against teachers

**Red Guards:** military units in high school/university that “eliminated class enemies” -> reign of terror
- More than 1 million people were killed due to them.
By **1967**, the movement led to mass violence, and factions competed for control in cities.
Eventually, Mao mobilized the PLA to send millions of Red Guards for re-education via labor in **1968**.

*Song Binbin was a poster girl of the Cultural Revolution, known for killing principal Bian Zhongyun.*

On **Feb 1971**, the US and China start to defrost relations, and on **October 1971**, the UN formally recognizes the PRC and kicks out the ROC/Taiwan from its seat.

Eventually Mao Zedong **dies** on **Sep. 1976**.

**Gang of Four** - CCP officials convicted for harsh policies
- crimes due to “ineffective leadership”

## Unit 8 (General)

### Deng Xiaoping’s Rise to Power
- After the fall of the Gang of Four, Deng arises as China’s leader in **December 1978**.

**”Communism with Mao Zedong Thought”** - Deng portrayed his reforms as developing Mao Zedong’s ideologies. He acknowledged the good and bad Mao did and aimed to apply Marxist principles to China to modernize it under the idea that Mao would have approved of it.
- Deng reinforced legitamacy of CCP
- Deng shifted economy towards growth
#### Policies:
- Four Modernizations of China - agriculture, industry, national defense, and STEM
- Replaced agricultural collectivization with government rented land
- One Child Policy, urbanization, students studied abroad.
	- As a result of students studying abroad, they were exposed to Western ideas.

**Special Economic Zones (SEZs):** designated coastal areas with capitalist-oriented policies to attract foreign investment.
- tax incentives, low labor costs, less government regulation on economy
Example: Shenzhen -> went from small fishing village to major global metropolis

#### Beijing Democracy Wall Movement (1978-1979):
- Often referred to as the Beijing Spring.
- Posters dispalyed on a long wall calling for economic reforms and human rights, criticizing Mao Zedong.
	- This spawned independent, unofficial journals for government news.
- As the wall started criticize core CCP ideals, Deng Xiaoping closed down the Xidan Wall.

#### Tianenmen Square Massacre (brief overview):
- April 1989 death of Hu Yaobang sparked initial mourning that turned into protests.
- Thousands of students occupied Tianenmen Square in protest.
- Deng decided to deploy the PLA on June 4, resulting in lots of bloodshed to restore order.
