#### Definition:
Detente - the easing of hostility or strained relations, especially between countries.

The US and USSR entered a period of detente after the Bay of Pigs and the Cuban Missile Crisis.

**1972, Strategic Arms Limitation Treaty (SALT):**
Richard Nixon went to the Soviet Union and visited Soviet leader Leonid Brezhnev to sign SALT. This aimed to freeze the number of ICBM's that each power could keep. 

**1972, Nixon's Visit to China**:
As Nixon visited China, the USSR became fearful that the U.S. and China would partner up, resulting in more cooperation between the Soviets and Americans to prevent isolation (**triangular diplomacy**). Nixon also did this because the American economy suffered from the Vietnam War.

**1975 Helsinki Accords:**
A massive diplomatic agreement signed by 35 nations (including US and USSR) for the USSR to respect human rights and basic freedoms and recognize post-WW2 borders of Eastern Europe.

**Trade of Grain:**
- The US gave excess grain to the Soviet Union, benefitting American farmers (access to a large, new market) and the people of the USSR.
- After the Soviets invaded Afghanistan in 1979, Jimmy Carter stopped grain shipments, ending detente.

### Soviet-Afghan War (1979-1989):
- The USSR wanted to raise communist influence against Muslim fighters in the region.
- They could not defeat the guerrilla groups in the rough terrain, thus undermining Soviet legitimacy.
- Around 562K to 2 million Afghani civilians died, and many fled to Pakistan and Iran.

### The Rise of US and USSR Tensions, Again
- Reagan sent military aid to support the Afghans against the USSR.
- Both the US and USSR had more than 12K missiles.

**Strategic Defense Initiative**:
- Reagan created a system that would destroy any Soviet nukes that attacked the United States or its allies.
- The Soviets lacked such a system and moderate Soviets started demanding reforms, having economic concerns and saw Reagan's initiative as the start to an arms race in space.
- Nations believed that they now had to choose sides between the US and USSR.
## <u>End of the Soviet Union</u>
- Mikhail Gorbachev rose to power in 1985, favoring perestroika (attempts to restructure economy) and glasnost (policy of opening of and increasing freedoms). Reagan particularly liked Gorbachev and met three times in two years.
- The Warsaw Pact started to fall as Gorbachev ended economic support to Soviet satellites in Eastern Europe. These countries started getting more exposed to Western democracy, leading to the **Fall of the Berlin Wall** in 1989.
- Lithuania, Georgia, and other Soviet republics started to overthrow their rulers, and the USSR dissolved in December 1991, forming many countries (the strongest being Russia).
