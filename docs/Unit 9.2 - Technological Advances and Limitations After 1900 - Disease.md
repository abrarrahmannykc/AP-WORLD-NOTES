
## Overview:
- New scientific and medicinal developments
- Pandemics: epidemic diseases that spread
internationally
- Diseases related to poverty (malaria, cholera,
tuberculosis), epidemics (HIV/AIDS, Ebola), and diseases
related to increased longevity (heart disease,
Alzheimers)

### 1918 Influenza Pandemic:
- 50M+ people killed, spread across the US (from Kansas)
- Spread globally as American troops arrived in France (cuz WW1 obv)
- Baseline Symptoms: fever, chills, sore throat, then newer symptoms like black feet, and "red lungs drowning in liquid"
- Called "Spanish Flu" because Spanish media was uncensored during WW1, so it reported accurate numbers and was thought of as the country of origin.
**Impact:** World economy collapsed because people aged 18-40 died of disease.
**Change:** Women joined the workforce, then gained the right to vote in 1920.

### HIV/Aids:

- Human Immunodeficiency Virus
- attacks cells that help fend off pathogens
- **Causes:** Unprotected sex, blood transfer, needles
- Not curable but treatable
- AIDS is a later stage of HIV that's more severe
- Antiretroviral drugs developed around 1990 to stop HIV but was expensive.
- Ethnic minorities, homosexual communities, and Southern African countries are most affected.

### Cholera:
- Bacterial disease spread via contaminated water.
- **Effects:** Forced countries to innovate better standard for cleaning water, and set up modern sanitation methods of water today.
- Prevalent in countries lacking WASH - water, sanitation, and hygiene
- Countries with environmental changes like floods are at greater risk.

### Alzheimers:
- **Nature**: A neurodegenerative disorder characterized by progressive memory loss and cognitive decline.
- **Biology**: Marked by extracellular Amyloid Beta plaques and intracellular Tau tangles.
- **History**: Originally thought to be a rare form of dementia until Robert Katzman’s 1976 paper identified it as a major killer and the 4th leading cause of death.

### Heart Disease:
- **Context**: Became the leading cause of death globally as life expectancy increased.
- **Causes**: Linked to genetics, lifestyle choices, smoking, and dietary changes.
- **Medical Advances**:
    - First heart transplant (1967) by Christiaan Barnard.   
    - First artificial heart (1982) by Robert Jarvik’s team.

### Ebola:
- **Source**: A virus originating in African fruit bats, transmitted to humans through fluids.
- **Outbreaks**: Significant spike in cases and deaths during the 2014 outbreak.

### Tuberculosis:
- **Nature**: A highly contagious bacterial infection typically affecting the lungs.
- **Symptoms**: Persistent cough (sometimes with blood), fever, night sweats, and weight loss.
- **Evolution of Treatment**:
    - _Early_: Quarantine and exposure to sunlight.
    - _Modern_: Use of specific antibiotics like Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, and Ethambutol.

### Malaria:
- **Cause**: A parasite spread by mosquitoes in tropical areas such as Africa and the Amazon.
- **Impact**: Kills over 600,000 people annually; historically, it accounted for 2–5% of all 20th-century deaths.
- **Demographics**: Predominantly affects young children under age 5 (accounting for ~75% of deaths).
- **Persistence**: Remains a threat due to poor housing, contaminated water, and limited healthcare in impoverished regions.
- **Prevention**: Includes mosquito nets treated with insecticide and vaccines.
