
# AP World History Unit 9.1: Advances in Technology and Exchange

## 1. The "Shrinking World" Concept

- **Definition**: The idea that while the physical size of the Earth remains constant, the time it takes to travel and communicate across it has decreased so drastically that it "feels" smaller.
    
- **Communication Technology**:
    
    - Early 1900s: Letters took weeks via ship.
        
    - Mid-20th Century: Radio and television spread news in real-time.
        
    - Late 20th/21st Century: The **Internet** and mobile phones created instant, global connectivity.
        
- **Transportation Technology**:
    
    - **Shipping Containers**: Standardized metal boxes that made it incredibly cheap and efficient to move goods globally, fueling the rise of global supply chains.
        
    - **Commercial Air Travel**: Reduced travel time between continents from weeks (by ship) to hours.
        

## 2. Energy Revolutions

- **Fossil Fuels**: Continued reliance on oil and coal powered the massive industrial growth of the 20th century.
    
- **Nuclear Power**: Emerged as a potent but controversial alternative for electricity after WWII.
    
- **Renewables**: Recent shifts toward solar and wind power in response to climate change.
    

## 3. The Green Revolution (Mid-20th Century)

- **Goal**: To increase food production and fight global hunger, particularly in developing nations.
    
- **Methods**: Development of high-yield seeds (GMOs), increased use of chemical fertilizers, and advanced irrigation.
    
- **Impact**:
    
    - **Positive**: Massive increase in world population and food security.
        
    - **Negative**: Environmental damage from pesticides, loss of genetic diversity in crops, and small farmers being pushed out by large agribusinesses.

# AP World History Unit 9.8 & 9.9: Global Institutions and Continuity/Change

## 1. Political Institutions: The United Nations (UN)

- **Purpose**: Founded in 1945 to replace the failed League of Nations. Aims to maintain international peace and security.
    
- **Key Components**:
    
    - **Security Council**: Can authorize military action (5 permanent members: US, UK, France, Russia, China).
        
    - **General Assembly**: Discussion body for all member nations.
        

## 2. Economic Institutions: The Bretton Woods System

- **IMF (International Monetary Fund)**: Provides short-term loans to prevent currency collapse.
    
- **World Bank**: Provides long-term loans for development projects in "Global South" nations.
    
- **WTO (World Trade Organization)**: Works to reduce tariffs and trade barriers (replaced GATT).
    

## 3. Global Security and Conflict

- **Post-Cold War Challenges**: Rise of international terrorism (e.g., **Al-Qaeda** and the **9/11 attacks**) led to new global security protocols and the "War on Terror."
    
- **Regional Blocs**: The **European Union (EU)** created a single market and currency (the Euro) to integrate Europe and prevent another world war.
    

## 4. Continuity and Change (Unit 9.9)

- **Continuity**: Humans still rely on trade, social hierarchies remain (though they are being challenged), and regional identities persist despite global culture.
    
- **Change**: The speed of interaction is unprecedented; the scale of environmental impact is now global rather than local; and non-state actors (MNCs, NGOs) now hold power traditionally reserved for governments.
