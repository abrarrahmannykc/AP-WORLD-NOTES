

## 1. The Big Picture: Empires in Collapse

As the 20th century dawned, the old-school land-based empires that had dominated global politics for centuries began to crack under the weight of internal economic stagnation, ethnic tensions, and a failure to modernize. By the 1920s, four major empires collapsed entirely, reshuffling the global balance of power.

## 2. Case Studies of Collapse

### A. The Russian Empire (1917)

- **The Root Causes**: Russia was slow to industrialize, leaving its massive peasant population impoverished. Political power was heavily centralized under the absolute rule of Tsar Nicholas II (Romanov Dynasty).
    
- **The Spark**: The devastation and food shortages of World War I pushed the population over the edge.
    
- **The Result**: The **Bolshevik Revolution (1917)**, led by Vladimir Lenin.
    
    - The Romanov dynasty was overthrown and executed.
        
    - The Bolsheviks established the world's first communist state, transforming Russia into the **Soviet Union (USSR)**.
        
    - **Economic Shift**: The government seized control of land, factories, and resources, replacing a weak capitalist economy with a state-directed command economy.
        

### B. The Qing Dynasty of China (1911)

- **The Root Causes**: Deep internal ethnic tension between the ruling Manchus (a minority) and the Han Chinese majority. Constant foreign intervention (via unequal treaties and Spheres of Influence) weakened the dynasty's legitimacy.
    
- **The Revolution**: The **Xinhai Revolution (1911)**, led by **Sun Yat-sen**.
    
- **The Ideology**: Sun Yat-sen championed the **Three Principles of the People**:
    
    1. _Nationalism_: Overthrowing Manchu rule and ending foreign imperialist intervention.
        
    2. _Democracy_: Establishing a constitutional republic.
        
    3. _Livelihood_: Ensuring economic security and land equalization for the peasantry.
        
- **The Sequel**: The fall of the Qing did not lead to immediate stability. Instead, China fractured into a chaotic **Warlord Era**, setting up a long internal conflict between the Chinese Nationalist Party (Kuomintang) and the rising Chinese Communist Party (CCP).
    

### C. The Ottoman Empire (1922)

- **The Root Causes**: Known as the **"Sick Man of Europe,"** the empire suffered from severe economic debt to Western banks and a failure to effectively industrialize. Heavy pushback from diverse ethnic minorities seeking independence (especially in the Balkans) tore the empire apart.
    
- **The Spark**: The Ottomans sided with the Central Powers (Germany and Austria-Hungary) in WWI and lost. The empire was partitioned by European powers via the Treaty of Sèvres.
    
- **The Rebirth**: Turkish nationalists, led by **Mustafa Kemal (Atatürk)**, fought a war of independence against foreign occupiers.
    
    - They abolished the Ottoman Sultanate and established the secular **Republic of Turkey** in 1923.
        
    - Atatürk launched radical modernization and westernization reforms: switching to the Latin alphabet, granting women legal rights, and stripping Islamic institutions of political authority.
        

### D. The Mexican Revolution (1910–1920)

- **The Root Causes**: Mexico was ruled by the authoritarian dictator **Porfirio Díaz**, whose policies heavily favored wealthy elites and foreign investors. Nearly 95% of rural peasants owned no land at all.
    
- **The Conflict**: A brutal, decade-long civil war broke out involving various revolutionary factions led by figures like Francisco Madero, **Emiliano Zapata** (who demanded _Tierra y Libertad_—Land and Liberty), and Pancho Villa.
    
- **The Resolution**: The adoption of the **Mexican Constitution of 1917**, which set a template for modern social reform:
    
    - **Land Reform**: Redistribution of land back to communal peasant groups (_ejidos_).
        
    - **Labor Rights**: Universal suffrage, minimum wage laws, 8-hour workdays, and the right to strike.
        
    - **Resource Control**: Stripped foreign corporations of natural resource ownership, stating that all subsoil rights belong to the Mexican nation.
