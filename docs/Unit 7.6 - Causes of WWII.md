World War II did not start in a vacuum. It was the direct consequence of unresolved issues from World War I, economic desperation from the Great Depression, and the aggressive expansion of totalitarian regimes.

## 2. The Legacy of Versailles & The League's Failure

The peace treaties of 1919 sowed the seeds for the next war by angering both the losers and some of the winners.

- **German Resentment**: The **Treaty of Versailles** completely humiliated Germany by forcing them to accept total blame for WWI, stripping them of territory, and imposing massive financial reparations. This tanked their economy and allowed Adolf Hitler to rise to power by promising to tear up the treaty.
    
- **The Failure of the League of Nations**: The international organization meant to prevent war proved completely toothless. It lacked an army, and major powers (like the United States) never joined. When aggressive nations broke international law, the League did nothing but issue weak verbal warnings.
    

---

## 3. Totalitarianism & Fascist Expansion

During the 1930s, militaristic and fascist dictatorships began aggressively conquering territory to build empires, directly defying the international community.

### Japan in Asia

- **The Goal**: Lacking natural resources of its own, Japan looked to conquer East Asia to feed its industrial economy.
    
- **The Actions**: In 1931, Japan staged an explosion on a railroad line (the Mukden Incident) as an excuse to invade **Manchuria** (Northern China). By 1937, they launched a full-scale invasion of mainland China, committing brutal atrocities like the Rape of Nanjing.
    

### Italy in Africa (Reviving Rome)

- **The Goal**: Fascist dictator Benito Mussolini wanted to restore the glory of the ancient Roman Empire.
    
- **The Action**: In 1935, Italy invaded **Ethiopia**. Despite the Ethiopian Emperor Haile Selassie pleading with the League of Nations for help, Western powers did practically nothing, allowing Italy to annex the country.
    

### Germany in Europe (_Lebensraum_)

- **The Goal**: Hitler wanted to unite all German-speaking people and conquer territory in Eastern Europe for **Lebensraum** ("living space").
    
- **The Actions**: Hitler systematically broke the Treaty of Versailles:
    
    - 1935: Rebuilt the German military.
        
    - 1936: Remilitarized the Rhineland (the border zone with France).
        
    - 1938: Annexed Austria (_Anschluss_) and demanded the Sudetenland (a German-speaking region of Czechoslovakia).
        

---

## 4. The Western Response: Appeasement

Instead of stepping in to stop Hitler, Great Britain and France chose a policy of **Appeasement**—giving in to an aggressor's demands in the hope that they will eventually be satisfied and stop.

- **The Munich Conference (1938)**: British Prime Minister Neville Chamberlain met with Hitler. To avoid another catastrophic world war, Britain and France agreed to let Hitler take the Sudetenland if he promised not to conquer anything else. Chamberlain famously declared he had secured "peace for our time."
    
- **The Twist**: Hitler completely ignored his promise and swallowed the rest of Czechoslovakia just months later.
    

---

## 5. The Final Countdown to War (1939)

Two shocking diplomatic moves officially triggered the outbreak of World War II:

1. **The Nazi-Soviet Non-Aggression Pact (August 1939)**: Bitter ideological enemies Adolf Hitler and Joseph Stalin shocked the world by signing a secret agreement promising _not_ to attack each other. Privately, they agreed to divide Poland between themselves. This protected Germany from fighting a two-front war.
    
2. **The Invasion of Poland (September 1, 1939) **: Free from the threat of a Soviet attack, Germany launched a massive invasion of Poland using its lightning-fast **Blitzkrieg** ("lightning war") strategy.
    

**The Result**: Realizing appeasement had failed, Great Britain and France finally drew a line in the sand and declared war on Germany. World War II had begun.
