
# AP World History Unit 7.2: Causes of World War I

## 1. The Underlying Causes: M-A-I-N

World War I didn’t just happen out of nowhere. Decades of tension built up a global powder keg. The easiest way to remember the long-term causes is the classic acronym **MAIN**:

```
 ───  M  ───►  MILITARISM      ───►  Massive industrial arms race
 ───  A  ───►  ALLIANCES       ───►  Secret treaties lock nations together
 ───  I  ───►  IMPERIALISM     ───►  Fierce global competition for colonies
 ───  N  ───►  NATIONALISM     ───►  Extreme pride & ethnic desire for independence
```

###  Militarism

- **What it was**: An aggressive glorification of military might and a massive buildup of weapons.
    
- **The Dynamic**: Driven by the Industrial Revolution, European powers competed to see who could amass the biggest, baddest military.
    
- **The Anglo-German Naval Race**: Germany, trying to establish itself as a dominant world power, started rapidly building up its navy. This directly threatened Great Britain, the traditional ruler of the seas. Both sides poured massive capital into building massive, heavily armored warships called **Dreadnoughts**.
    

###  Alliances

- **What it was**: Treaties binding countries to defend one another if attacked. Intended to act as a deterrent to war, they instead acted as a tripwire.
    
- **The Two Blocs**:
    
    - **Triple Entente**: Great Britain, France, and Russia.
        
    - **Triple Alliance**: Germany, Austria-Hungary, and Italy (though Italy later flipped sides).
        

###  Imperialism

- **What it was**: The scramble for overseas colonies, market control, and raw materials.
    
- **The Dynamic**: European powers had spent the late 19th century carving up Africa and Asia (think Berlin Conference). By 1914, there was almost no land left to conquer, so empires began clashing over existing boundaries, driving mutual suspicion to an all-time high.
    

### Nationalism

- **What it was**: Intense pride in one's country or ethnic group, often coupled with the belief that your group is superior to others.
    
- **The Balkans**: Known as the **"Powder Keg of Europe."** This region was a volatile mix of diverse ethnic groups. The Austro-Hungarian Empire controlled much of this territory, but Slavic nationalist groups (especially Serbians) fiercely wanted independence to form a unified Slavic state.

## 2. The Spark: The Assassination of Franz Ferdinand

While **MAIN** laid out the dry kindling, a single assassination lit the match.

- **The Target**: Archduke Franz Ferdinand, heir to the Austro-Hungarian throne.
    
- **The Action**: On June 28, 1914, while visiting Sarajevo, Bosnia, the Archduke and his wife were shot and killed.
    
- **The Assassin**: **Gavrilo Princip**, a 19-year-old Serbian nationalist and member of a radical group called the **Black Hand**.
    
 
### The July Crisis Domino Effect
> 
> 1. **Austria-Hungary** blames Serbia for the assassination and issues a harsh ultimatum.
>     
> 2. **Serbia** refuses to comply fully.
>     
> 3. **Austria-Hungary** declares war on Serbia.
>     
> 4. **Russia** (sharing Slavic ethnic ties) mobilizes its military to defend Serbia.
>     
> 5. **Germany** views Russian mobilization as an act of war and declares war on Russia and its ally, **France**.
>     
> 6. **Great Britain** joins the fight when Germany marches through neutral Belgium to invade France.
