
## 1. The Global Economy After WWI: 

The roaring twenties didn't roar everywhere, and they definitely didn't last. The massive financial cost of World War I, combined with poor economic planning, threw the world into the **Great Depression** by 1929.

### Germany's Hyperinflation 

- **The Problem**: The Treaty of Versailles forced Germany to pay massive war reparations. Germany didn't have the money, so the government simply printed more.
    
- **The Result**: **Hyperinflation**. The German Mark became completely worthless. People literally used stacks of cash as building blocks for kids or burned money to keep their houses warm because it was cheaper than buying wood.
    

### 📉 The U.S. Stock Market Crash (1929)

- **The Spark**: On "Black Tuesday" (October 29, 1929), the U.S. stock market collapsed due to over-speculation and buying stocks on margin (borrowed money).
    
- **The Ripple Effect**: Because the U.S. had been lending money to European nations to rebuild, the American crash pulled the rest of the global economy down with it. Global trade plummeted by over 60%.

## 2. Competing Economic Fixes

Nations realized _laissez-faire_ (hands-off) capitalism wasn't fixing the crisis. Governments had to step in. Three radically different economic models emerged:

### Model A: The New Deal (Regulated Capitalism)

- **Who**: U.S. President Franklin D. Roosevelt (FDR).
    
- **The Strategy**: Inspired by **Keynesian Economics**, which argues that during a recession, the government _must_ spend money to jumpstart the economy.
    
- **The Actions**:
    
    - Created massive public works projects (building dams, roads, and bridges) to employ millions.
        
    - Introduced safety nets like **Social Security** and FDIC banking regulations to prevent future crashes.
        

### ☭ Model B: The Five-Year Plans (State Command Economy)

- **Who**: Joseph Stalin in the Soviet Union (USSR).
    
- **The Strategy**: Total government control over production. Stalin completely rejected capitalism.
    
- **The Actions**:
    
    - **Collectivization of Agriculture**: The state seized private farms to create massive, government-run mega-farms. Peasants who resisted (like the _Kulaks_) were executed or sent to gulags. This chaos directly triggered catastrophic artificial famines, like the **Holodomor** in Ukraine.
        
    - **Heavy Industrialization**: Total focus on factories, steel production, and mining. While brutal, it transformed the USSR from a backwards agrarian society into an industrial powerhouse in record time.
        

### Model C: Fascist Corporate Economies

- **Who**: Benito Mussolini (Italy) and Adolf Hitler (Germany).
    
- **The Strategy**: A hybrid approach. Private property and corporations were allowed to exist, _but_ they had to serve the goals of the fascist state.
    
- **The Actions**: Extreme militarism. Both regimes poured massive amounts of state money into illegal rearmament (building tanks, planes, and weapons) and public infrastructure (like the German Autobahn) to artificially eliminate unemployment.
