
### Unit 8.3 - Effects of the Cold War
After the [[Cuba and the Cold War#<u>Cuban Missile Crisis</u> |Cuban Missile Crisis]], the US, USSR, and 100 other nations signed the **Nuclear Test-Ban Treaty (1963),** outlawing nuclear weapon testing in:
- space
- underwater
- above ground
	- Note: France and China *did not* sign it.

**1968 - Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty**
- an international agreement aiming to prevent the spread of nuclear weapons and technology to non-nuclear nations.

In fact, there were also people that supported an **antinuclear weapons movement.**
- **Japan (1954)** opposed US testing of nuclear weapons in the Pacific. A year later, more than 1/3rd of the population signed a petition against nukes.
- The **antinuclear weapons movement** spread to western nations in the late 1970s and early 1980s.
	- **New York City (1982)** - One million people demonstrated against the creation and use of nukes.
